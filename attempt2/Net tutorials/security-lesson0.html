Lesson 0: Cyber Security

Introduction

What is cyber security? You might initially think of people hacking computers or machines to access information or make machines do things. What it actually is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. In this lesson, you will learn the basic terms and concepts in cyber security.

Types of Hackers

There are many types of hackers included in this field. Some are good and some are bad or both. It's important to know the types of hackers so you could safely navigate the internet and work with to avoid legal troubles.

White Hat Hackers

  • Ethical Hacking
  • Security Testing Team
  • Improve Security Systems

Black Hat Hackers

  • Malicious hacking techniques
  • Unauthorized Access Techniques
  • Desire for stolen data, using the data maliciously, or damaging it intentionally

Grey Hat Hackers

  • Utilizing both types of hacking methods interchangeably
  • They often break rules but not necessarily with any evil intentions
  • Sometimes they expose security lapses to claim recognition or influence change

What are some benefits to this field?

  • Protects Sensitive Information: Prevents unauthorized access to personal, financial, and business data.
  • Builds reliability: Enhances customer and stakeholder confidence in the organization's or a businesses ability to protect data.
  • Prevents Financial Loss: Reduces the risk of financial losses due to cyber attacks.
  • Protects Intellectual Property: Secures proprietary information and trade secrets from theft.
  • Supports National Security: Guards against cyber espionage and attacks on critical infrastructure.

What is a Cyber Attack?

A cyber attack is an attempt by hackers to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, or devices. These attacks can be executed using various methods and tactics.

Effects of Cyber Attacks

  • Financial Losses: Direct financial losses due to theft of funds, ransomware payments, and loss of revenue from disrupted operations.
  • Loss of Data: Destruction or theft of sensitive information, including intellectual property and customer data.
  • Damage to the organization's reputation, resulting in loss of customer trust and loyalty.

Notable examples of Cyber Attacks

1. Stuxnet (2010)

A sophisticated worm targeted Iran's nuclear facilities which caused physical damage to centrifuges used in uranium enrichment by altering their speeds. This significantly set back Iran's nuclear program. The attack demonstrated the potential for cyber attacks to cause physical damage to critical infrastructure.

2. Sony Pictures Hack (2014)

A cyber attack from North Korea that led to the theft and release of confidential data, including unreleased films and employee information in the company. This led to major financial losses, reputational damage, and operational disruption for Sony Pictures. The attack highlighted the vulnerabilities in corporate cybersecurity and the potential for politically motivated cyber attacks.

3. WannaCry Ransomware Attack (2017)

A global ransomware attack that affected over 200,000 computers in 150 countries by exploiting a vulnerability in Microsoft Windows. The attack caused widespread disruption, particularly in the UK's National Health Service (NHS), and led to estimated damages in the billions of dollars. As a result of the attack, it emphasized the importance of timely software updates and the global reach of cyber threats.

How to become certified in Cyber Security

Education: You should start by first obtaining a degree in computer science, information technology, or a related field.

Certificates:

  • CompTIA Security+: Covers basic cybersecurity concepts and skills. This certificate is good for those who are starting in this field.
  • Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP): Requires at least five years of work experience in at least two of the eight CISSP domains. This certificate is one of the more recognized certificates in the field.

Prepare for the Exam

Study Materials: Use official study guides, books, online resources like websites and mock exams to prepare for exams for certificates. Many certificate companies provide official study materials.
